Benkart, Georgia(1-WI); Sottile, Frank( 3-TRNT); Stroomer, Jeffrey
Tableau switching: algorithms and applications. (English. English summary)
J. Combin. Theory Ser. A, 76 (1996), no. 1, 11--43.
The aim of this paper is to introduce a new operation, called switching, that takes a pair of Young tableaux $(S,T)$ such that the outer border of $S$ coincides with the inner border of $T$, and moves them through each other, yielding a pair $(S\sb T,{{}\sp S}T)$ such that the outer border of ${{}\sp S}T$ coincides with the inner border of $S\sb T$. It is shown that this operation is unique if one wants it to satisfy some natural constraints. Moreover, similarly to the jeu de taquin, the switching procedure consists in elementary steps which can be performed in several ways without affecting the final result. As particular cases, the authors recover algorithms introduced by Haiman, Shimozono, James-Kerber and Remmel. Applications are given to symmetric function identities. A final section discusses relationships between switching and evacuation.
