Huber, Birkett (1-MSRI); Sottile, Frank (1-WI); Sturmfels, Bernd (1-CA)
Numerical Schubert calculus. (English. English summary)
Symbolic numeric algebra for polynomials.
J. Symbolic Comput. 26 (1998), no. 6, 767--788.
Suppose we are given linear subspaces $K\sb 1,\cdots, K\sb n$ of $\bold C\sp {m+p}$ with $\dim K\sb i=m+1-k\sb i$ and $k\sb 1+\cdots +k\sb n=mp$. The problem is to find all p-dimensional linear subspaces of $\bold C\sp {m+p}$ which meet each $K\sb i$ nontrivially. When the given linear subspaces are in general position, there is a finite number $d=d(m,p,k\sb 1,\cdots, k\sb n)$ of such $p$-planes.

The objective of this paper is to present semi-numerical algorithms for computing all d solution planes from the input data $K\sb 1,\cdots, K\sb n$. Two homotopy algorithms which treat the important special case $k\sb 1=\cdots=k\sb n=1$ are given. The first algorithm is derived from a Grobner basis for the Plucker ideal of a Grassmannian and the second from a SAGBI basis for its projective coordinate ring. (For definitions see B. Sturmfels [Grobner bases and convex polytopes, Amer. Math. Soc., Providence, RI, 1996; MR 97b:13034].) Both the Grobner homotopy and SAGBI homotopy are techniques for finding linear sections of Grassmannians in their Plucker embedding.

In Section 3, the authors describe a numerical method for solving the general case of our problem. This is the method for solving the polynomial equations defined by special Schubert conditions. Here a sequence of delicate intrinsic deformations, called Pieri homotopies, which were introduced in a paper by F. Sottile [Canad. J. Math. 49 (1997), no. 6, 1281--1298; MR 2000b:14070], is applied.

Some computational results and applications of these algorithms to control theory and real enumerative geometry are discussed.
