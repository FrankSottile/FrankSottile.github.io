Discriminant Conjecture 26
Clearly, the discriminant should be positive on monotone choices of parameters.
Based on the previous example and a few others, we conjecture something much stronger.

Discriminant Conjecture
The discriminant of a polynomial system modeling Grassmannian conditions on partial flags imposed by osculating flags lies in any preorder generated by the differences s-t of the parameters defining a monotone choice of points.

  • Again, by the asymptotic result, this would imply the Monotone Conjecture.
  • We have computed some examples where the preorder of polynomials positive on a monotone set of points is not finitely generated, but the discriminant has the form of the conjecture.
