Coamoeba of a symmetric line in 3-space
    The coamoeba of the highly symmetric line in (C*)3,
t --> (t-1, t-ζ, t-ζ2),
(ζ is a primitive third root of 1) is a 2-dimensional membrane whose boundary consists of four lines, one parallel to each of the coordinate directions. (It contains no points of these lines.)

    It does contain a number of other lines, and its projection in any coordinate direction is the two triangles that we saw of a line in the plane.
Eight fundamental domains.
