  • The reading is short, but a bit hard. Fermat is discussing his "little Theorem" in examples. Here is some of the mathematics going on. 33-1 is a multiple of 13, which implies that 36-1 is a multiple of 13. This is because 36-1=(33-1)(33+1). The same reasoning gives that 312-1 is a multiple of 13, which is an instance of Fermat's little theorem.
        Note how hard it is to understand what he is doing; he has a very different way of expressing himself, and does not use algebraic notation. This topic, perfect numbers, and Pell's equation are all discussed in the course Math 629: History of Mathematics.
