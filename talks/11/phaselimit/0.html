Coamoeba of a line in the plane
    The coamoeba of the line x+y+1=0 in (C*)2 consists of those pairs (θx, θy) for which there exist a triangle whose sides have these angles.
In the fundamental domain [-π, π]2, the closure of this coamoeba is two triangles without their edges, but with their vertices.
