Sottile, F. (1-MSRI)
The special Schubert calculus is real. (English. English summary)
Electron. Res. Announc. Amer. Math. Soc. 5 (1999), 35--39 (electronic).
Let $G$ be the Grassmannian of complex $k$-dimensional subspaces of ${\bold C}\sp n$. In this note the author shows that for any enumerative problem on $G$ given by an intersection of special Schubert cycles, there exist real cycles for which all of the (a priori complex) solutions are real. The fixed planes which define the special Schubert conditions are chosen osculating a rational normal curve at real points.

The short proof uses two results of D. Eisenbud and J. D. Harris [Invent. Math. 74 (1983), no. 3, 371--418; MR 85h:14019]. The author notes that his theorem provides evidence for a more general conjecture of B. Shapiro and M. Shapiro, which involves arbitrary Schubert conditions. He also gives an application to the problem of placing real poles in linear systems theory.
