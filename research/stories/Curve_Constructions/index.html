Real Plane curves: Constructions
  • The subject of real algebraic plane curves began in 1876, when Harnack proved that a smooth real plane curve of degree d can have at most 1 + (d-1)(d-2)/2 ovals, and then he constructed plane curves of any degree achieving this bound. Harnack's inductive construction is very elegant and straightforward.
    A sequence of web pages illustrating Harnack's construction of his sextic.

  • Much work was done on finding prohibitions as well as new constructions in the ensuing 100 years. A breakthrough with important consequences for other fields was achieved by Viro around 1980 with his construction of T-curves based upon what are now called toric deformations.
