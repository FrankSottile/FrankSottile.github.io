Intersection theory on spherical varieties. (English)
J. Algebraic Geom., 4 (1995), no. 1, 181--193.
This paper studies algebraic cycles on a variety X with an action of a connected solvable, affine algebraic group G. Denote by AkX the group of k-dimensional cycles up to rational equivalence; define AGkX to be the group of k-dimensional, \Gamma-stable cycles modulo the equivalence relation generated by divisors of \Gamma-semi-invariant rational functions, in \Gamma-stable subvarieties of dimension k+1. Then the natural map AGkX --> AkX is an isomorphism for all k \geq 0 (Theorem 1). When X is projective, this is due to A. Hirschowitz [C. R. Acad. Sci. Paris Ser. I Math. 298 (1984), no. 5, 87--89; MR 85j:14007] and his result is used in the paper under review. If the number of G-orbits in X is finite, then the canonical map A*X \otimes A*Y --> A*(X\times Y) is an isomorphism for any scheme Y (Theorem 2). Other results concern the "Chow cohomology groups" A*X [W. Fulton, Intersection theory, Springer, Berlin, 1984; MR 85k:14004 (Chapter 17)]. For complete X, there is an evaluation map AkX --> Hom(AkX,Z). This map turns out to be an isomorphism when G acts on X with finitely many orbits (Theorem 3). If moreover G is a torus (i.e. X is a toric variety), then the action of A*X on A*X and the cup-product in A*X are described in Theorem 4, in combinatorial terms. This is developed in further work of W. Fulton and B. Sturmfels ["Intersection theory in toric varieties", Topology, to appear]. On the other hand, Theorems 2 and 3 have been generalized by B. Totaro ["Chow groups, Chow cohomology, and linear varieties", J. Algebraic Geom., to appear].
