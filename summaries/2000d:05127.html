Bergeron, Nantel (3-YORK-MS); Sottile, F. (1-WI)
Schubert polynomials, the Bruhat order, and the geometry of flag manifolds.
Duke Math. J., 95, (1998), no. 2, pp. 373--423.
Schubert polynomials ${\germ S}\sb w$ were defined by A. Lascoux and M.-P. Schutzenberger [C. R. Acad. Sci. Paris Ser. I Math. 294 (1982), no. 13, 447--450; MR 83e:14039] as distinguished representatives of Schubert cycles in the cohomology ring of a flag manifold. When $w$ runs over the group ${\scr S}\sb\infty$ of all finite permutations, then the polynomials $ {\germ S}\sb w$ form a basis of the ring ${\bold Z}[x\sb 1,x\sb 2,\cdots]$ of polynomials in infinitely many variables. Let $c\sb{uv}\sp w$ be structure constants in that basis defined by the equation ${\germ S}\sb u {\germ S}\sb v =\sum\sb w c\sb{uv}\sp w{\germ S}\sb w$. Since Schubert polynomials generalize Schur polynomials, multiplication rules for $ {\germ S}\sb w$ should generalize the Littlewood-Richardson rule, but for general permutations $u,v$ the multiplication rule remains unknown. The authors search for a combinatorial interpretation of the constants $c\sb{uv}\sp w$ as numbers of some saturated chains in the Bruhat order on ${\scr S}\sb\infty$. Starting from an analysis of the Monk formula for multiplication by ${\germ S}\sb v$, where $v$ is a simple transposition, they establish some properties of the Bruhat order and find a recursion relation for the coefficients $c\sb{uv}\sp w$ in some special cases.
